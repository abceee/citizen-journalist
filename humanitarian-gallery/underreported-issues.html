Underreported issues

Stories the world should know more about

There are many humanitarian stories that do not attract media attention. Chronic or long-term issues tend to lose in the fight for space in print media as new headlining stories come in.

Plus, there are not enough journalists devoted to covering humanitarian situations especially involving aid relief efforts.

From time to time, humanitarian organisations like the United Nations and Médecins Sans Frontières (Doctors Without Borders) would release a list of what it deem to be the top humanitarian crises that are largely unreported in media.

The most recent Doctors Without Borders report on underreported humanitarian issues came out in 2009 and among the “Top Ten” include:

  • Civilians getting killed and neglected amid long-running conflicts between the army and opposition groups in Pakistan.
  • Lack of funds to treat over 10 million people afflicted with HIV/AIDS in developing countries.
  • About 3.5 million children dying from malnutrition every year due scarce funding for treatment.

The UN’s Department of Public Information listed humanitarian issues that it said hardly garnered media attention such as the following:

  • The need to create strategies to combat climate change reaching critical point as the planet rapidly warms.
  • More people are going hungry in poor nations while food prices climb sharply, triggering riots in 40 countries as the global food crisis continues.
  • Even space debris is fast becoming a critical issue, as it threatens sustainable use of outer space.
