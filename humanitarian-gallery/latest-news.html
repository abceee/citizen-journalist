Latest news & pictures

Check out the latest humanitarian news and view pictures of recent and previous disasters that have reached humanitarian emergency proportions.

  • Egyptian violence

    United Nations human rights chief Navi Pillay called for an independent investigation into the reported use of force of Egypt's military against protesters, who are demanding a return to civilian rule following the ouster of Hosni Mubarak as president earlier this year.

  • Libya conflict

    Libya forms Cabinet but faces tough challenges in restoring peace and order in the country not long after Muammar Gadhafi's 42-year-rule ended with his death in October at the height of the Libyan uprising.

  • Global peace and security

    Senior officials of the United Nations named organised crime, pandemics and the impacts of climate change as the most threatening challenges to global peace and security, as the Security Council hold a debate on these issues this month.

  • Southeast Asia floods

    A lone powerful cyclone bearing the international name “Nalgae” affected three countries in its path namely, the Philippines, China and now Thailand. While it is now weakening, it left over 80 people dead in the Philippines and hundreds more in Thailand.

  • Aids report

    The latest report on the global AIDS epidemic shows encouraging results amid a decrease in infection rates and deaths related to HIV. The report is produced by the Joint UN Programme on HIV/AIDS.

  • Disease outbreak

    A deadly outbreak of acute watery diarrhoea has spread across the Horn of Africa with drought, lack of hygiene and persistent regional conflict as the major contributing factors cited by the United Nations and World Health Organisation.

  • Tsunami warning system

    To avoid a disaster similar to the Indian Ocean tsunami, over 20 nations participated in an exercise to test the “UN-backed early warning system” that reenacted events of the 2004 global tragedy.
