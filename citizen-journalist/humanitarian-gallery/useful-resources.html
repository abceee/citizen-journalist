Useful resources

How do you know when a crisis is really a crisis? How can you sense that an emergency is already unravelling?

These are just a few questions that even professional journalists need help with.

And here’s where humanitarian aid agencies are particularly a useful resource. But don’t just get their opinion and that’s it – ask what it is based on and double-check your information.

Here are some useful resources to check out:

Humanitarian news websites

  • United Nations News Centre
  • Thomson Reuters Foundation’s AlertNet
  • Internews
  • Integrated Regional Information Networks (IRIN), a service of the UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs
  • ReliefWeb

Facts and figures

  • ReliefWeb’s country profiles and updates on disasters
  • AlertNet’s country profiles and interactive statistics
  • UN Statistics Division
  • UN Data Site

Climate change & disaster preparedness

  • Red Cross/ Red Crescent Centre on Climate Change and Disaster Preparedness
  • UN International Strategy for Disaster Reduction
  • Prevention Web
