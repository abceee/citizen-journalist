Asking focused questions

When disaster strikes whether natural or man-made, the first question that comes to mind is why did it happen?

The question becomes more pressing when it results in loss of lives, displacement of people and destruction of homes and structures.

The basics

Was it really climate change?

Asking if climate change caused a disaster or humanitarian situation cannot be avoided. However, avoid loosely attributing every natural disaster or freakish weather event to it.

Assess the factors first that caused the disaster by looking into reliable sources of information from humanitarian organisations like the United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change or humanitarian news websites like AlertNet.

Many of these organisations have readily accessible and easy to grasp data on climate change and other humanitarian topics.

Could the disaster or loss of lives have been avoided?

Earthquakes are unpredictable but the disaster takes on a whole new meaning when the loss of lives reaches humanitarian proportions.

Sample Situation: In the 2008 massive earthquake in the Sichuan province of China, some 70,000 people were killed including thousands of children when their schools collapsed. Building codes and standards were questioned.

Who is to blame?

What efforts are being made to prevent disasters?

This involves not just governments but communities and the humanitarian aid industry.

It can tackle partnerships being forged for the common goal of providing a safer and sustainable environment.

What consultations are aid agencies conducting with locals to make them more prepared? Are you familiar with how these consultations go and what they accomplish?

How has a disaster affected relevant industries?

Tourism, for example, is one industry that almost immediately feels the impact of a natural disaster especially if the place is prone to it like cyclones and flooding in Queensland, Australia.

Getting more in-depth

What other disaster factors are we overlooking?

Consider this: The rapid growth of towns and cities, or urbanisation, is increasingly becoming a concern for the United Nations as it fuels the risks of a disaster.

How are these cities addressing this concern?

What are the other possible impacts of climate change?

Beyond floods, cyclones and earthquakes, experts predict more migration of so-called climate refugees, food and water shortages, outbreaks of disease and conflict as a result of climate change.

Can you find the connection?
